THE LAST MESSAGE

FADE IN:

INT. CRAMPED APARTMENT - NIGHT

A single lamp cuts through the dark. MEERA (32), dark circles under her eyes, sits hunched over a laptop. Empty coffee cups crowd the desk.

Her phone buzzes. She ignores it. It buzzes again.

MEERA
(muttering)
Not now.

She types. Stops. Reads what she's written. Deletes it all.

The phone screen lights up — the message preview reads: "I know what you found."

Meera goes still.

She picks up the phone slowly, as if it might bite. Full message:

"I know what you found in the files. Meet me. Come alone. Tell no one. You have two hours."

No sender name. Unknown number.

Meera closes the laptop. Opens it again. Pulls up a folder labeled "ARCHIVED — DO NOT OPEN."

Inside: spreadsheets. Financial records. Names she recognizes.

Her hands start to shake.

CUT TO:

INT. PARKING GARAGE - LEVEL 3 - NIGHT

Meera steps out of the stairwell. Fluorescent lights flicker overhead. The garage is empty — or looks it.

Her footsteps echo too loud.

VOICE (O.S.)
You came alone?

Meera spins. RAJAN (50s), silver-haired, expensive coat, steps from behind a concrete pillar. She knows him. Everyone knows him.

MEERA
Mr. Rajan. I—

RAJAN
(cutting her off)
How much did you see?

MEERA
Enough.

A long silence. Rajan studies her face like he's reading a balance sheet.

RAJAN
Then you understand why I can't let you leave here with that information.

He nods toward the shadows. A SECOND FIGURE steps forward.

Meera's phone — still in her hand — she presses SEND on a draft she prepared before leaving home.

MEERA
(quietly)
Already sent.

Rajan's expression doesn't change. But something shifts behind his eyes.

RAJAN
Sent to whom?

MEERA
Everyone who matters.

Beat.

RAJAN
You're bluffing.

MEERA
Check your phone.

Rajan reaches into his coat pocket. Slowly. His hand pauses.

SMASH CUT TO BLACK.

The sound of a single notification chime.

FADE OUT.
