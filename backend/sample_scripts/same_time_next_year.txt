SAME TIME NEXT YEAR

FADE IN:

INT. SMALL BOOKSHOP - AFTERNOON

Rain against the windows. The shop is quiet, warm, smells like old paper.

PRIYA (28) stands on a step stool, reaching for a book on the top shelf. She's almost got it when —

The shelf shifts. Books avalanche. She grabs the nearest thing — which turns out to be the arm of ARJUN (30), who catches her by reflex, dropping the stack of books he was carrying.

They end up face to face. Very close. Neither moves.

PRIYA
You can let go now.

ARJUN
Right. Yes.

He doesn't.

PRIYA
(almost amused)
Any time.

ARJUN
(finally releasing her)
Sorry. I — you were falling.

PRIYA
I had it.

ARJUN
You were absolutely falling.

She looks at the pile of books on the floor. Looks back at him.

PRIYA
Fine. Thank you.

They both crouch to pick up the books at the same time. Their hands reach for the same novel.

ARJUN
Nabokov?

PRIYA
It's a gift. For a friend.

ARJUN
Which one?

PRIYA
Pnin.

ARJUN
Good choice. Better than Lolita for a gift.

PRIYA
(surprised)
Most people don't know Pnin.

ARJUN
Most people haven't spent three years being the only outsider in a department full of literature professors.

A beat. She tilts her head.

PRIYA
What department?

ARJUN
Physics. It was a very confusing time.

She laughs — genuinely, not politely. He looks caught off guard by how much he likes the sound.

ARJUN (CONT'D)
I'm Arjun.

PRIYA
Priya.

They're still crouched on the floor, surrounded by fallen books, rain on the windows.

ARJUN
Do you want to get coffee? After you're done — I mean, I don't mean right now, I just —

PRIYA
I'm done now.

ARJUN
Oh.

PRIYA
(standing, brushing herself off)
There's a place across the street. They have terrible coffee.

ARJUN
I love terrible coffee.

PRIYA
(already walking toward the door)
I know.

ARJUN
(calling after her)
How do you know?

PRIYA
(without turning)
You were in here last Tuesday buying a book about string theory. Anyone who voluntarily reads about string theory has low standards for beverages.

Arjun stares after her. Smiles slowly to himself.

He follows.

FADE OUT.
